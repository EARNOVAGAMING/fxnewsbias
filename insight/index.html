How These Insights Work

Every insight starts with the same raw data: thousands of forex news headlines published by sources including FXStreet, ForexLive, Action Forex, and Reuters. The FXNewsBias sentiment engine scores each headline for currency impact, then aggregates those scores into a single 0–100 reading for each of the 8 major currencies — USD, EUR, GBP, JPY, AUD, CAD, CHF, and NZD.

Each insight explains which currency led the session, what the three main news drivers were, and what scenarios traders should watch over the following 24 hours. The Asia wrap publishes at 00:00 UTC, the London wrap at 06:00 UTC, and the New York wrap at 12:00 UTC — Monday to Friday only. Weekends are skipped because forex markets are closed and no material news flow is processed.

Insights are most useful alongside the live currency strength meter and the economic calendar. Together they give you the news-driven bias, the current score, and the upcoming events that could shift it.

Asia Session

London Session

New York Session

US data releases — NFP, CPI, Fed decisions — land in this window. USD and CAD are primary; cross-market risk sentiment drives everything. Often the most volatile session of the day.

Reading the Sentiment Score

Each currency is scored from 0 to 100 based on how bullish or bearish the news flow has been over the most recent analysis window. The scale works as follows:

  • 70–100 — Strong Bullish: Consistent positive news flow. Currency is a candidate for long positions against weaker counterparts.
  • 55–69 — Mild Bullish: Leaning positive but not decisively. Worth monitoring for confirming data before acting.
  • 45–54 — Neutral: No clear directional bias from news. Range-trading conditions are likely.
  • 31–44 — Mild Bearish: Leaning negative. Short setups may develop if selling pressure accelerates.
  • 0–30 — Strong Bearish: Consistent negative news flow. Currency is a candidate for short positions against stronger counterparts.

The widest gap between two currency scores points to the highest-conviction news-driven setup. A USD score of 78 against a GBP score of 24, for example, gives a 54-point gap — historically a high-conviction fundamental case for GBP/USD short.
