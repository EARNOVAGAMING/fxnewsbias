USD Rallies to 160 Handle on Fed Repricing & Stock Market Records

New York is opening — here is the forex sentiment setup heading into the US session. US Dollar (USD) leads forex sentiment today with a strong bullish reading. Here is what drove the move and what to watch next.

What Happened

The US Dollar extended its rally Friday on two pillars of strength: persistent carry support anchored in high US yields, and renewed appetite for risk assets following President Trump's stock market record announcement. MUFG highlighted that carry support remains intact on elevated yields, underpinning demand for the greenback even as geopolitical tensions simmer in the Middle East. Simultaneously, the Trump tax policy announcement boosted equities to record highs, lifting risk appetite and dollar appeal across the forex market analysis landscape. This dual foundation—yield attraction and equity optimism—created a rare window where the USD could strengthen on both fundamental and sentiment grounds.

Meanwhile, Fed repricing expectations have shifted decisively hawkish. Markets are re-evaluating the terminal rate path, with traders pricing in a stickier inflation narrative and a more patient Fed. This repricing dynamic, outlined by Forex Crunch's analysis of USD/JPY price action, pushes international investors toward dollar-denominated assets. The yen, by contrast, faces headwinds from Japan's weak macro backdrop and BoJ reticence, leaving the carry trade heavily skewed in favour of dollar longs versus yen shorts.

Market Reaction

The broader currency market has bifurcated sharply: the USD has printed gains across nearly all majors, while the yen has borne the brunt of dollar strength, pushing USD/JPY toward the psychologically critical 160 level despite BoJ intervention risks and close government coordination. FXStreet reported that the Japanese Yen neared this key threshold, a level that typically triggers official pushback from Tokyo. Yet even coordinated jawboning from BoJ Governor Ueda and Prime Minister Takaichi—who agreed to continue close coordination—has failed to arrest the yen's decline, underscoring the market's conviction in the dollar's structural advantages.

The widest sentiment gap emerged between the USD (72/100 bullish) and the JPY (35/100 bearish), a 37-point spread that reflects the acute asymmetry in monetary policy expectations and yield differentials. EUR and AUD also softened, as dovish repricing in the ECB and RBA contrasted with hawkish Fed bets, turning these pairs into secondary winners for dollar strength. GBP traded defensively higher despite fiscal headwinds, while CAD and NZD remained range-bound, unable to break free from consolidation zones.

What's Driving the Move

Three key threads run through the bullish US Dollar story:

  1. Carry support persists on high US yields, with MUFG identifying persistent yield attraction as a foundational driver of dollar demand amid Fed repricing expectations.
  2. Trump's stock market record announcement boosted risk appetite and equity valuations, lifting the greenback's appeal as markets price in pro-growth fiscal policy.
  3. BoJ and Japanese government coordination efforts have failed to arrest yen weakness, leaving USD/JPY vulnerable to further upside as policy divergence widens.

What to Watch Next

📈 Bull case for the move
A break above USD/JPY 160 with follow-through closing above 161 would signal a capitulation of BoJ intervention barriers and open the door to 165. Alternatively, hotter-than-expected US CPI or PCE data next week would re-anchor Fed terminal rate expectations even higher, extending carry trades and dollar strength. Technical confirmation above the 160 psychological level, combined with fresh hawkish Fed commentary, could ignite a sustained push into the mid-160s.
📉 Risk to the view
A coordinated BoJ intervention campaign—or a surprise yen-supportive policy announcement—could reverse the trend abruptly, as it did during prior episodes of explicit yen defense. If US equities roll over or Trump's tax plan encounters legislative headwinds, risk appetite could evaporate quickly, eroding the dollar's equity-linked bid and flipping carry positioning. Additionally, a surprisingly dovish Fed speaker or softer-than-expected US inflation print would undercut yield support and force dollar shorts to cover at more attractive levels.

Watch for Asia-Pacific session developments Sunday evening and Monday open, where BoJ officials may reiterate intervention warnings and Japanese policymakers could attempt fresh verbal guidance on currency stability.
