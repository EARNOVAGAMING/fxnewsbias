USD Hits 40-Year High in USD/JPY Above 163 on Iran Tensions & Oil Rally

Asia session is opening — here is the overnight forex sentiment picture as Tokyo, Singapore and Sydney desks come online. US Dollar (USD) leads forex sentiment today with a strong bullish reading. Here is what drove the move and what to watch next.

By FXNewsBias Team·Published Wed, 22 Jul 2026·4 min read

US Dollar (USD) surges to 75/100 bullish as Middle East tensions and safe-haven demand lift the greenback to one-week highs.

Learn why geopolitical risk, crude oil gains, and USD/JPY's fresh 40-year peak above 163 are reshaping FX positioning in the Asia session.

What Happened

The US Dollar Index hit one-week highs on Wednesday as escalating US-Iran conflict and the threat of a Houthi blockade intensified safe-haven demand for the greenback. US attacks on Iran continued for an 11th consecutive night, triggering a fresh wave of risk-aversion that benefited the dollar over riskier assets. Crude oil climbed to a five-week high as Saudi tankers were forced to reroute due to blockade threats, further cementing the geopolitical premium and boosting dollar-denominated commodity hedging flows.

The standout price action came in USD/JPY, which pierced through 163.00 to reach a fresh 40-year high as equities, US Treasury yields, and oil prices all climbed in tandem. This three-pronged rally—stocks, bonds, and commodities moving higher simultaneously—typically occurs when inflation concerns and geopolitical risk dominate market psychology. The yen, traditionally a safe-haven currency itself, weakened sharply as US yields rose faster than Japanese rates, pulling the carry trade back into favor and allowing the dollar to extend its dominance against Tokyo.

Market Reaction

The forex market responded with sharp directional conviction, with the US Dollar strength creating the widest sentiment gap of the session: USD at 75/100 bullish versus the Japanese Yen at just 28/100 bearish—a 47-point spread reflecting the magnitude of dollar dominance. EUR/USD pulled back off session highs to 1.1402 as the European single currency struggled to maintain footing against a resurgent greenback, while the Australian Dollar consolidated above 0.7000 despite the broader dollar rally, suggesting some regional demand cushioning AUD weakness.

Currency pairs tied to commodity exporters bore the brunt of the move. The Canadian Dollar fell to a fresh low as tariff headwinds persisted and oil gains failed to offset trade policy risk, leaving CAD at 35/100 deeply bearish. GBP/JPY held above its year-to-date 218.00 peak even as sterling received a decent UK jobs report, underscoring how cross-yen pairs are now primarily tracking dollar momentum rather than local economic data. The Swiss Franc held neutral footing at 58/100, held steady by its own safe-haven credentials but unable to compete with the dollar's broader appeal.

What's Driving the Move

Three key threads run through the bullish US Dollar story:

  1. US Dollar Index climbed to one-week highs as US-Iran military tensions intensified, with attacks continuing for 11 consecutive nights and triggering fresh safe-haven demand.
  2. USD/JPY broke above 163.00 to hit a 40-year high as US Treasury yields rose faster than Japanese rates, widening the carry-trade appeal and accelerating yen weakness.
  3. Oil surged to a five-week high on reports that Houthi blockade threats forced Saudi crude tankers to turn back, driving both geopolitical premiums and dollar-denominated hedging flows.

What to Watch Next

📈 Bull case for the move
A continuation of Middle East tensions without direct escalation to US soil would allow the dollar's safe-haven bid to persist through London and New York sessions. If crude oil maintains its five-week high above $84 and US Treasury yields hold above current levels, USD/JPY could test the 164.00 handle, drawing fresh momentum from positioning. Any official confirmation that Iran will retaliate would likely extend the dollar rally as risk-off flows accelerate.
📉 Risk to the view
An abrupt de-escalation announcement—such as a ceasefire agreement, diplomatic breakthrough, or explicit statement that further US strikes have ended—would rapidly deflate the geopolitical premium and allow risk sentiment to recover. In such a scenario, oil would collapse, yields would fall, and carry pairs like USD/JPY could snap lower by 200+ pips as traders unwind leveraged long-dollar positions. Chinese Yuan strength near 6.7600 could also signal central bank support for risk appetite, pulling capital away from dollar safe-haven trades.

Watch for fresh headlines on Iran and Houthi activity heading into London's open, where European real-money flows may test the dollar's resilience against geopolitical noise.
