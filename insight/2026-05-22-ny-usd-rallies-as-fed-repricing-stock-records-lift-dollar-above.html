USD Rallies as Fed Repricing & Stock Records Lift Dollar Above JPY 160

What Happened

The US Dollar rallied decisively on Friday as two powerful tailwinds converged: persistent carry support underpinned by elevated US Treasury yields and fresh repricing of Federal Reserve expectations. According to MUFG analysis cited in FXStreet, carry support remains robust despite recent volatility, anchoring demand for the currency. Simultaneously, the forex market absorbed President Trump's stock market record announcement, which boosted risk appetite and underscored confidence in US fiscal policy, further cementing the greenback's appeal as both a yield play and a risk-on proxy.

The combination of these drivers—high real rates and equity optimism—created a powerful bid for dollars throughout the session. Rather than fragmenting flows, the dual narrative (yield attraction plus equity strength) pulled in both carry traders and risk-positive investors, a rare alignment that amplified USD/JPY and broader greenback strength. Market participants repriced Fed hold-or-cut probabilities downward, implying longer duration of elevated rates, which extended the dollar's term premium edge over major peers.

Market Reaction

Across the eight major currencies, the FX session crystallised into a clear USD strength narrative with one standout loser: the Japanese Yen fell to 35/100 bearish, creating the session's widest sentiment divergence. USD/JPY held firm above the psychologically critical 160 level despite acknowledged intervention risks and close government coordination signals from BoJ Governor Ueda and Prime Minister Takaichi. The yen's inability to stabilise near that barrier—even as authorities publicly flagged readiness to defend it—underscored how powerful the Fed carry narrative has become relative to BoJ caution.

Meanwhile, the Australian Dollar (38/100) and Euro (42/100) both retreated into bearish territory as hawkish RBA bets faded and ECB messaging split between Demarco's June hike signal and Lagarde's data-dependent rhetoric. British Pound held neutral (55/100) despite UK fiscal stress, and both the Canadian Dollar and Swiss Franc remained range-bound at 52 and 50 respectively, with no fresh catalysts to dislodge them. The greenback's outperformance was not driven by broad risk-off repricing but rather by a structural reassessment of US monetary policy duration.

What's Driving the Move

Three key threads run through the bullish US Dollar story:

  1. MUFG confirmed that carry support persists on elevated US Treasury yields, attracting both leveraged and institutional dollar demand in a high-rate environment.
  2. President Trump announced a new stock market record, lifting broader risk appetite and equity valuations, which boosted USD appeal as equities rallied and volatility stayed contained.
  3. Japanese BoJ Governor Ueda confirmed close coordination with government on economic and market events but offered no new stimulus, leaving the yen structurally weak against rising US yields.

What to Watch Next

📈 Bull case for the move
A bullish USD scenario would require confirmation that Fed terminal rates remain higher for longer, perhaps signalled by hotter-than-expected US inflation data or hawkish FOMC member commentary in the next 48 hours. Technical acceptance above 160.50 in USD/JPY with a failed BoJ intervention attempt would seal conviction and trigger fresh long positioning across carry trades. If equity markets sustain record highs without a sharp correction, the risk-on bid for dollars will persist.
📉 Risk to the view
The dollar's rally could reverse sharply if economic data (US jobless claims, manufacturing PMI, or consumer sentiment) signals recession fears, forcing a sudden repricing of Fed easing into Q4 2026. A coordinated BoJ verbal or actual intervention effort near 160.00, coupled with explicit government pressure to weaken the yen, might spook dollar longs and trigger unwinding of carry positions. Alternatively, any geopolitical escalation (e.g. fresh Iran tensions spiking oil past $100) could pivot the narrative from yield-led dollar strength to safe-haven flows benefiting CHF and gold instead.

Watch the Asia and London opens for any BoJ officials' weekend commentary or fresh Japan fiscal policy signals that could reshape the yen's intervention risk calculus.
