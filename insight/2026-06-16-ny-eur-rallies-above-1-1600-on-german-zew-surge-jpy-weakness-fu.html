EUR Rallies Above 1.1600 on German ZEW Surge, JPY Weakness Fuels EUR/JPY

Euro (EUR) rises to 64/100 bullish as German economic sentiment turns decisively positive, lifting the currency above 1.1600 and establishing it as today's session leader.

What Happened

The Euro gained meaningful ground on Tuesday after Germany's ZEW Economic Sentiment index jumped to 10.5 in June, swinging into positive territory for the first time in several months. FXStreet's coverage of the German economic sentiment data highlighted the turnaround as a critical boost for EUR positioning, with the currency edge up above 1.1600 in the New York session. The shift reflects easing Middle East tensions following a US-Iran nuclear agreement breakthrough, which had weighed on risk sentiment and energy prices in prior sessions.

EUR strength was further underpinned by a broader risk-on backdrop. Oil prices extended losses as the US lifted its naval blockade, reducing safe-haven demand and allowing growth-sensitive currencies like the Euro to outperform traditional safe havens. The combination of brighter German economic data and a pullback in geopolitical risk created a constructive environment for euro bulls, positioning the currency to challenge resistance levels that had capped it during the earlier phases of the week.

Market Reaction

The broader forex market reacted to EUR's rally by reassessing risk appetite and positioning ahead of major central bank decisions. The New York session saw the Dollar hold its ground at 62/100 bullish, but the widest sentiment divergence emerged between the Euro's 64/100 and the Japanese Yen's 38/100 bearish score—a 26-point gap that underscores a classic risk-on squeeze favoring commodity-linked and cyclical currencies over traditional safe havens.

EUR/JPY emerged as the session's standout pair, with the Euro benefiting from positive economic data while the Japanese Yen remained hamstrung by a BoJ rate hike that failed to provide meaningful support. BBH analysts noted that gradual Bank of Japan tightening continues to keep the Yen under pressure, creating a two-way flow: EUR gains from genuine growth optimism while JPY deteriorates from intervention risk and carry-trade dynamics. Risk-sensitive currencies like the New Zealand Dollar and Australian Dollar both lagged, their sub-50/100 scores reflecting lingering concerns over dovish central bank bias and softer commodity demand.

What's Driving the Move

Three key threads run through the bullish Euro story:

  1. Germany's ZEW Economic Sentiment survey jumped to 10.5 in June, turning positive and directly supporting Euro valuation across major pairs.
  2. Bank of Japan rate hike continuation fails to prevent Japanese Yen weakness, creating a structural headwind for JPY and an asymmetric tailwind for EUR/JPY cross flows.
  3. Easing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and sub-$78 WTI oil prices reduce safe-haven demand, allowing growth-sensitive currencies like EUR to outperform while commodity exporters remain range-bound.

What to Watch Next

📈 Bull case for the move
A break and hold above 1.1650 in EUR/USD would confirm the ZEW bounce and attract fresh momentum buyers into the New York close and Asia open. If the BoJ signals further patience on rate hikes in coming weeks—or if US inflation data next week disappoints—the Fed repricing trade could accelerate EUR gains as carry unwind pressure on the Yen intensifies.
📉 Risk to the view
A sharp reversal in oil prices (WTI bouncing above $80) on renewed Hormuz supply concerns would flip risk sentiment back to safe-haven flows, favoring the Dollar and Yen over the Euro. Alternatively, any hawkish surprise from the BoE decision or a surge in UK wage data could pull capital away from EUR/GBP shorts and undermine the broad Euro rally momentum.

Watch for Asia-Pacific open confirmation of EUR/JPY strength; any hesitation below 1.1600 in EUR/USD would suggest the ZEW relief is already priced in.
