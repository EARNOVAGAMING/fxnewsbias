AUD Rallies Above 0.7000 on 76.3k Jobs Beat, RBA Rate-Hike Signals

Australian Dollar (AUD) surges to 74/100 bullish on stronger-than-expected jobs growth and RBA rate-hike signals.

What Happened

The Australian Dollar extended its rally through the London session after domestic jobs data delivered a significant beat. The Aussie labour market printed 76.3k new positions, exceeding economist forecasts and suggesting the Reserve Bank of Australia still has room to tighten policy at its August meeting. This employment surprise reignited bets that the RBA may not be finished hiking, providing fundamental underpinning for AUD pairs after weeks of dovish central bank repricing globally.

The currency strength materialized in AUD/USD, which built on overnight gains and cleared the psychologically important 0.7000 level. Traders repositioned long positions as the jobs report countered recent narratives of economic slowdown in Australia, allowing the Aussie to attract carry and momentum flows. Against a backdrop of mixed USD performance—the greenback holding near 101.00 despite risk aversion—the Australian Dollar's outperformance reflects a divergence in monetary policy trajectories rather than broad-based currency weakness elsewhere.

Market Reaction

The broader FX session pivoted around central bank divergence, with hawkish antipodean narratives standing out sharply against a dovish Fed repricing trade. The New Zealand Dollar mirrored AUD's strength, climbing above 0.5800 on RBNZ hawkish guidance, creating a unified ANZ outperformance story. Meanwhile, the euro advanced ahead of the ECB decision as oil reignited inflation concerns, suggesting that rate-hike expectations are regaining traction across multiple policy regions.

The widest sentiment gap emerged between AUD/USD (74/100 bullish) and CAD (48/100 neutral), as commodity-linked antipodean currencies benefited from both hawkish central bank positioning and near-term technical momentum, while the Canadian Dollar faced cross-currents from dovish Fed repricing offsetting oil's six-week highs. The most tradeable setup lay in AUD pairs against lower-yielding currencies, particularly EUR/AUD and GBP/AUD, where the exchange rate divergence between the Aussie's tightening bias and the relative stability of Old World central banks offered tactical long opportunities.

What's Driving the Move

Three key threads run through the bullish Australian Dollar story:

  1. Australian jobs data released 76.3k new positions, significantly beating forecasts and keeping the RBA's August rate hike door open according to FXStreet.
  2. AUD/USD price action cleared and held above the 0.7000 psychological level, triggering momentum-driven buying from both carry traders and technical breakout participants.
  3. Oil holding near six-week highs around $87.50 supported commodity-linked currency demand, reinforcing the Aussie's safe-haven appeal relative to dovish central banks.

What to Watch Next

📈 Bull case for the move
A sustained break above 0.7050 would confirm the bullish structure and potentially unlock further gains toward 0.7150 if upcoming RBA speaker commentary reaffirms rate-hike expectations. Any softening in US inflation data over the next 48 hours would further weaken the USD and extend AUD's outperformance, especially if it reduces near-term Fed rate-hike odds.
📉 Risk to the view
A sharp reversal in oil prices below $85 would undermine commodity-driven AUD demand and challenge the 0.7000 support level. Alternatively, if the RBA signals a more dovish stance than markets currently price, or if Australian wage data disappoints at the next release, the currency strength could unwind rapidly as rate-hike expectations collapse.

Watch for Asian session opens to confirm whether AUD momentum persists or consolidates, as offshore demand and Chinese risk appetite will shape the next leg of price action.
