What Is Driving the NZD Today

The New Zealand Dollar — affectionately called the "kiwi" after the national bird — is the smallest of the G10 majors by FX trading volume. That smaller liquidity means NZD often moves more violently than its peers when news hits, and spreads can widen quickly during quiet hours. NZD is a commodity currency like AUD and CAD, but its export mix is dominated by dairy products rather than minerals or oil.

NZD direction is shaped by three things: what the RBNZ is signaling about rates, what global dairy auction prices are doing, and how the Chinese and Australian economies are performing (NZ's two biggest trading partners). The kiwi tends to track AUD closely most of the time, but can decouple sharply on RBNZ decision days or after major Global Dairy Trade auctions.

Quick read: NZD has the widest spreads and lowest liquidity of the majors — avoid trading it during the Asia-late-night window unless you accept slippage risk.

Reserve Bank of New Zealand — The Central Bank Behind the NZD

The Reserve Bank of New Zealand was the first central bank in the world to adopt formal inflation targeting (in 1990) and remains one of the most influential institutions in monetary policy circles. The RBNZ holds 7 policy meetings per year, with a Monetary Policy Statement at 4 of them. Governor Adrian Orr is known for being direct and sometimes blunt in his communications, which can produce sharp NZD moves on his comments.

What the RBNZ watches most

  • New Zealand quarterly CPI (the RBNZ's primary inflation read)
  • Global Dairy Trade (GDT) auction results — every 2 weeks
  • New Zealand employment report and wage data (quarterly)
  • RBNZ Monetary Policy Statement — published at 4 of the 7 yearly meetings
  • China and Australia data — leading indicators for NZ exports

What Moves the NZD Most

These are the catalysts that move NZD the most:

  • RBNZ rate decisions and Monetary Policy Statements — biggest single mover; expect 80–150 pip moves on NZD/USD
  • NZ quarterly CPI inflation — four times a year so each release is heavily weighted
  • Global Dairy Trade auctions — every 2 weeks; sustained dairy price moves push NZD over time
  • Chinese economic data — NZ exports a lot of dairy and meat to China — Chinese demand matters
  • Risk events — NZD is risk-on like AUD, sells off during global stress

Common Questions About the NZD

Is the New Zealand Dollar bullish today?
Check the live card at the top of this page. The score updates every 3 hours and incorporates RBNZ commentary, NZ data, dairy auction results and Chinese demand signals.
Why is NZD so volatile?
Because it has the smallest trading volume of the G10 majors. Less liquidity means each order moves the price more, so news events that would move EUR/USD 30 pips can move NZD/USD 80 pips.
When does NZD move most?
9pm GMT (Wellington open) for NZ data, RBNZ decision time at 1am or 2am GMT depending on daylight saving, and dairy auction Tuesdays at around midnight GMT.
Does NZD always move with AUD?
Most of the time yes — both are commodity currencies exposed to China. But on RBNZ decision days they often diverge, and AUD/NZD can move 1%+ on a hawkish RBNZ surprise.
Why is NZD called the "kiwi"?
Because the New Zealand one-dollar coin features a kiwi bird (the national bird of New Zealand) on the reverse. The nickname has been standard trader slang for decades.
